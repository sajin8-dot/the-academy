# Agentic Design Patterns

The biggest shift in AI for 2026 is the move from "Single-Shot" prompts to **"Agentic Workflows."** Instead of asking a model to do something once and accepting the first answer, we now build systems that iterate, reflect, and collaborate.

## The Four Pillars (The Andrew Ng Framework)

To build a sharp agentic ecosystem, you must master these four patterns:

### 1. Reflection
The model critiques its own work. 
- **The Loop:** Brains generate → Checker critiques → Brains refine.
- **Why it works:** It forces the model to look at its own "blind spots" before you ever see the output.

### 2. Tool Use
The agent stops guessing and starts doing.
- **The Loop:** Model identifies missing data → Calls `web_search` or `exec` → Incorporates real facts.
- **Why it works:** It grounds the reasoning in reality rather than training data.

### 3. Planning
Breaking the "Big Problem" into "Small Deltas."
- **The Loop:** Brains analyze the goal → Create a step-by-step roadmap → Execute one step at a time.
- **Why it works:** It prevents the model from getting overwhelmed by complexity.

### 4. Multi-Agent Collaboration
Assigning specific roles (The Team Roster).
- **The Loop:** Scout (Muscle) finds → Maker (Brains) builds → Auditor (Brains) verifies.
- **Why it works:** Specialization beats generalism every time.

## Practical Application: The "Hard No" Orchestration

In our own ecosystem, we apply **Planning** and **Collaboration** by strictly tiering our models:
- **Muscle (Flash-Lite):** The Scout (Tool Use).
- **Talent (Flash):** The Synthesizer (Reflection).
- **Brains (Pro):** The Architect (Planning).
