# Lesson 1: The Ear-First Foundation

Music is not a series of ink blots on paper; it is the spontaneous language of the collective unconscious. To teach reading before hearing is to cage the bird before it learns it has wings.

We begin here. Not with the eyes, but with the soul’s receptor: the ear.

[START DRONE: frequency=261.63, volume=0.4, waveform="warm-pad"]

## The Center of Gravity

Most teachers start by pointing at a white key and calling it "C." They give you a letter, a box, a category. But a note isn't a category. It's a relationship. It's a feeling of rest or a feeling of longing.

Imagine you are standing on a steep hill. Gravity is pulling you. You feel it in your knees, in your core. Music has that same gravity. Every song has a "Center of Gravity"—a place where the sound feels safe, finished, and at home. We call this the **Tonic**.

Before you learn its name, you must learn its weight.

## The Search: Finding 'Home'

Listen to the drone. It’s a warm, vibrating hum filling the room. It is waiting for its partner.

### Exercise 1: The First Bloom
"Reach out and press any white key. Don't look. Just feel the vibration."

[AWAIT MIDI: target="C4", timeout=null]
[ON SUCCESS: BLOOM intensity=high, color="gold"]
[ON FAILURE: feedback="That note has tension... it's a question. Find the answer. Find the rest."]

**The Scribe:** "Do you hear how the room suddenly feels stable? The clashing stops. That is your Home Key. It is your center of gravity."

## Ear-to-Hand: The Neighborhood

Now that you've found the center, let's explore the immediate neighborhood. Do these without looking at your hands.

### Exercise 2: The Gravity Pull
"Listen to this 'ache'—a note that desperately wants to come home."

[PLAY MIDI: sequence=["D4"], instrument="piano"]
"Now, you play it. Then, bring it home."

[AWAIT MIDI: sequence=["D4", "C4"], tolerance=0.1]
[ON SUCCESS: BLOOM intensity=medium, color="cyan"]
[ON FAILURE: feedback="You're wandering. Listen for the ache, then the relief. Try again."]

**The Scribe:** "The distance between the tension and the rest is where the story lives."

### Exercise 3: The Three-Note Walk
"I'll play the first three steps of a journey. Listen to the climb."

[PLAY MIDI: sequence=["C4", "D4", "E4"], instrument="piano"]
"Now, walk up those three steps with me. Let your fingers find the distance the ear already knows."

[AWAIT MIDI: sequence=["C4", "D4", "E4"], timeout=15000]
[ON SUCCESS: BLOOM intensity=high, color="green"]
[ON FAILURE: feedback="The steps are even, like a heartbeat. If you're lost, find Home (the Tonic) and start the climb again."]

## Cobain's Reflection
We are so obsessed with "getting it right" that we forget to "feel it true." If you hit a "wrong" note today, don't wince. Listen to it. Ask it why it's there. Every dissonance is just a resolution waiting for its turn to speak.

In the next session, we will map the geography of this soundscape. For now, just stay in the hum. Stay at home.
